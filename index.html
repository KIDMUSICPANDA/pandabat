BIOGRAPHY / PROFILE

PandaBat is the solo project of Marthin Chan, a producer and songwriter based in Miami. Built around mood, restraint, and emotional texture, PandaBat lives in the space between post-punk and indie pop, where songs unfold slowly and atmosphere takes priority over spectacle.

The project explores themes of intimacy, melancholy, and emotional distance, drawing influence from the darker romanticism of Joy Division and The Cure, alongside the melodic pulse of classic alternative and new wave. Sonically, PandaBat favors minimal arrangements, steady basslines, and understated guitars—music that feels personal, unresolved, and quietly immersive.

While rooted in Chan’s production and songwriting, PandaBat is designed as a collaborative platform, frequently working with female vocalists and artists who bring their own emotional perspectives into each release. Rather than a single narrative voice, the project functions as a shared emotional space shaped by different energies, tones, and stories.

PandaBat doesn’t aim to chase trends or define conclusions. It exists as a reflective outlet—music meant to be felt rather than explained, inviting listeners to linger in emotion rather than escape it.
